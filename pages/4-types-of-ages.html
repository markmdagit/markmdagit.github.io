4 Types of Ages

Physical Age (The Chronological Clock)

Concept: A person who is 30 years old who runs marathons often may have a heart of a 22 year old, while a 30 year old with a poor lifestyle may have the joints of a 45 year old.

Mental Age (The Intellectual Clock)

Concepts: Crystallized vs Fluid Intelligence

  • Crystallized Intelligence: The facts, college degrees, and life lessons that are "stored" in your mind.
  • Fluid Intelligence: The ability to solve new problems.

Example: A person can be 20 years old but have a mental age of a 40 year old if they have undergone extreme hardships or intense specialized education, giving them a "stored library" of knowledge or experiences that is far beyond their age.

Social Age (The Relational Clock)

Concept: Interpersonal Maturity, or the ability to regulate emotions, and express empathy.

  • Child Stage: Communication is "I want/I need", and is primarily ego driven.
  • Adult Stage: Communication is more "We", and involved active listening, conflict resolution, and ability to read social cues.

Example: A physical adult (age 50+) that has a "Social Age" of a 10 year old when they throw a tantrum after not getting their way.

Spiritual Age (The Existential/Temporal Clock)

Concepts: Historical Resonance and The Cosmic Perspective

  • Historical Resonance: The idea of the "Old Soul" that favors an era before their time (Someone who focuses on the 1900s or the Middle Ages).
  • Cosmic Perspective: The ability for a person to look at the "Big Picture" and how they will be perceived through time and space.
